Business Studies

Competencies

  • Understand business environments and entrepreneurship.
  • Apply financial literacy skills in real-life scenarios.
  • Analyze business operations and markets.
  • Develop business plans and make decisions ethically.
  • Use ICT tools for business management and communication.

Notes

Business Environment: Types of businesses, markets, customers, stakeholders.
Entrepreneurship: Business ideas, planning, startups, risk management.
Financial Literacy: Budgeting, accounting basics, savings, investment.
Marketing: Product, price, place, promotion, customer service.
ICT in Business: Digital payments, online marketing, spreadsheets, record keeping.

Activities / Experiments

  1. Design a simple business plan for a small venture.
  2. Create a budget for a week using real-life scenarios.
  3. Conduct a simple market survey and record results.
  4. Simulate business transactions using mock money.

Quizzes

  1. List the four P’s of marketing.
  2. Explain the importance of budgeting in a business.
  3. Identify stakeholders in a given business scenario.
  4. What are three qualities of a successful entrepreneur?

Projects

  • Start a mini-business at school and track its performance.
  • Create a poster advertising a product or service.
  • Analyze the costs and benefits of a small business idea.

Reflection

Reflect on how business knowledge can help manage personal finances and future career opportunities.

Geography

Competencies

  • Understand physical and human geography and their interactions.
  • Analyze environmental and societal issues using maps and data.
  • Apply sustainable practices in daily life.
  • Interpret diagrams, maps, and charts accurately.
  • Use ICT for geographic research and presentation.

Notes

Physical Geography: Landforms, weather, climate, ecosystems.
Human Geography: Population, settlements, transport, agriculture, industry.
Environment: Pollution, deforestation, conservation, sustainable development.
Maps & Data: Topography, scale, longitude/latitude, contour lines.
GIS & ICT: Mapping software, data analysis, presentation tools.

Activities / Experiments

  1. Draw a map of your school environment using scale.
  2. Record weather data for one week and analyze trends.
  3. Identify different landforms in your locality and describe their uses.
  4. Investigate environmental issues in your community and suggest solutions.

Quizzes

  1. Define climate and weather.
  2. Identify four major landforms in Kenya.
  3. Explain the difference between renewable and non-renewable resources.
  4. Interpret a simple population chart of a town.

Projects

  • Create a model showing a landform and its uses.
  • Develop a presentation on an environmental issue in your area.
  • Collect data about transport networks in your town and display diagrammatically.

Reflection

Reflect on how geographic knowledge helps in planning, sustainability, and understanding human-environment interaction.

History & Citizenship

Competencies

  • Understand key historical events and their impact.
  • Analyze causes and effects of societal changes.
  • Promote civic responsibility and ethical citizenship.
  • Interpret historical evidence and sources.
  • Apply lessons from history to modern life.

Notes

History: Ancient civilizations, colonial history, independence movements.
Civic Education: Rights and responsibilities, democracy, governance.
Ethics: Human rights, social justice, ethical decision-making.
Sources: Primary and secondary sources, oral histories, documents.

Activities / Experiments

  1. Analyze historical documents and identify bias.
  2. Discuss civic responsibilities in class debates.
  3. Interview elders about historical events.
  4. Visit local heritage sites and record observations.

Quizzes

  1. List three ancient African civilizations.
  2. Explain the significance of independence movements in Kenya.
  3. Define democracy and its key features.
  4. Identify examples of human rights violations in history.

Projects

  • Create a timeline of major historical events in Kenya.
  • Write an essay on the importance of civic responsibility.
  • Prepare a presentation on a historical figure and their contributions.

Reflection

Reflect on how understanding history and citizenship contributes to informed and responsible societal participation.

Sports Science

Competencies

  • Understand principles of physical fitness and health.
  • Apply knowledge of nutrition and exercise in daily life.
  • Analyze performance and techniques in various sports.
  • Promote teamwork, leadership, and ethical sportsmanship.
  • Use ICT to track fitness and progress.

Notes

Fitness: Components of fitness, exercises, warm-up, cool-down.
Health & Nutrition: Balanced diet, hydration, injury prevention.
Sports Skills: Techniques, rules, strategies in different games.
Performance Analysis: Measuring improvement, recording results.
ICT in Sports: Fitness apps, wearable devices, performance tracking.

Activities / Experiments

  1. Measure pulse rate before and after exercise.
  2. Perform and record basic fitness tests (e.g., shuttle run, push-ups).
  3. Analyze performance in a chosen sport using metrics.
  4. Plan a weekly exercise and nutrition schedule.

Quizzes

  1. List the components of physical fitness.
  2. Explain the importance of hydration during exercise.
  3. Describe proper warm-up and cool-down routines.
  4. Identify ways to prevent common sports injuries.

Projects

  • Develop a personal fitness improvement plan.
  • Prepare a report analyzing a team’s performance.
  • Create an infographic on nutrition and exercise.

Reflection

Reflect on how sports science knowledge helps maintain fitness, health, and overall well-being.

Visual Arts

Competencies

  • Develop creativity through artistic expression.
  • Use different media and techniques effectively.
  • Analyze and appreciate artworks from different cultures.
  • Plan and execute art projects responsibly.
  • Reflect on personal and societal messages in art.

Notes

Drawing & Painting: Techniques, perspective, color theory.
Sculpture: Materials, form, texture, 3D representation.
Art History: Local and global art movements, famous artists.
Design Principles: Balance, proportion, rhythm, emphasis.
Media: Pencil, charcoal, watercolor, clay, digital tools.

Activities / Experiments

  1. Create a still-life drawing using shading techniques.
  2. Design a sculpture using recycled materials.
  3. Analyze a famous painting and describe its elements.
  4. Experiment with color mixing to produce new shades.

Quizzes

  1. Define perspective in drawing.
  2. List three types of sculpture materials.
  3. Name a famous artist and one of their works.
  4. Explain the importance of balance and proportion in design.

Projects

  • Create a portfolio of drawings and paintings.
  • Design a poster or logo for a school event.
  • Develop a sculpture or 3D project.

Reflection

Reflect on how visual arts develop creativity and communicate ideas.

Performing Arts

Competencies

  • Develop skills in music, dance, and drama.
  • Understand performance techniques and expression.
  • Analyze and appreciate performances.
  • Collaborate effectively in group performances.
  • Use creativity to communicate messages through performing arts.

Notes

Music: Rhythm, melody, harmony, instruments, composition.
Dance: Movements, styles, coordination, expression.
Drama: Acting, improvisation, scriptwriting, stage presence.
Performance Analysis: Critique, reflection, audience engagement.
Collaboration: Teamwork, rehearsal, leadership, roles.

Activities / Experiments

  1. Practice a short musical piece using an instrument or voice.
  2. Create a short dance routine and perform for peers.
  3. Perform a short drama scene and analyze performance.
  4. Record and critique your performance for improvement.

Quizzes

  1. List three types of dance styles.
  2. Define melody, rhythm, and harmony in music.
  3. Explain the role of rehearsal in drama performance.
  4. Identify key elements of stage presence.

Projects

  • Organize a small school performance (music, dance, or drama).
  • Create a short original musical composition or script.
  • Collaborate with peers to produce a performance video.

Reflection

Reflect on how performing arts help develop confidence, creativity, and communication skills.
