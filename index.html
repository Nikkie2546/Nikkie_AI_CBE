Mathematics

Competencies

  • Apply algebraic techniques to solve real-world problems.
  • Use geometry to analyze shapes and measurements in daily life.
  • Interpret statistical data and represent it diagrammatically.
  • Perform calculations involving financial literacy and percentages.
  • Use ICT tools for problem-solving in mathematics.

Notes

Algebra: Simplifying expressions, solving linear and quadratic equations, factorization, substitution.
Geometry: Angles, triangles, quadrilaterals, circles, Pythagoras theorem, coordinate geometry.
Statistics: Mean, median, mode, frequency tables, bar graphs, histograms, pie charts.
Financial Maths: Interest calculations, percentages, ratios, budgeting exercises.
Problem Solving: Applying mathematical reasoning to real-life situations.

Activities / Experiments

  1. Measure classroom objects and calculate area/volume.
  2. Collect class data and represent it using pie charts and bar graphs.
  3. Solve real-life word problems involving percentages and ratios.
  4. Use a spreadsheet to compute statistics and create graphs.

Quizzes

  1. Solve: 2x + 5 = 17
  2. Calculate the area of a triangle with base 8 cm and height 5 cm.
  3. Convert 25% into a fraction and decimal.
  4. Create a bar graph for the following data: [10, 15, 12, 20]

Projects

  • Design a mini-budget for a week using percentages and ratios.
  • Collect data from your environment (e.g., heights of classmates) and represent it diagrammatically.
  • Create a real-life word problem and solve it step-by-step.

Reflection

Write a short reflection on how mathematical skills can be applied in everyday life and future career paths.

Business Studies

Competencies

  • Understand business environments and entrepreneurship.
  • Apply financial literacy skills in real-life scenarios.
  • Analyze business operations and markets.
  • Develop business plans and make decisions ethically.
  • Use ICT tools for business management and communication.

Notes

Business Environment: Types of businesses, markets, customers, stakeholders.
Entrepreneurship: Business ideas, planning, startups, risk management.
Financial Literacy: Budgeting, accounting basics, savings, investment.
Marketing: Product, price, place, promotion, customer service.
ICT in Business: Digital payments, online marketing, spreadsheets, record keeping.

Activities / Experiments

  1. Design a simple business plan for a small venture.
  2. Create a budget for a week using real-life scenarios.
  3. Conduct a simple market survey and record results.
  4. Simulate business transactions using mock money.

Quizzes

  1. List the four P’s of marketing.
  2. Explain the importance of budgeting in a business.
  3. Identify stakeholders in a given business scenario.
  4. What are three qualities of a successful entrepreneur?

Projects

  • Start a mini-business at school and track its performance.
  • Create a poster advertising a product or service.
  • Analyze the costs and benefits of a small business idea.

Reflection

Reflect on how business knowledge can help manage personal finances and future career opportunities.
